Top 10 Real Detectives

Detectives are known for their ability to solve complex cases and bring criminals to justice.

Here are the top 10 real detectives and their most famous cases, as well as any unsolved cases that they may have encountered.

List of 10 Real Detectives

  1. Sherlock Holmes
  2. Allan Pinkerton
  3. William Burns
  4. Joseph Wambaugh
  5. Frank Abagnale
  6. Ed Parker
  7. Kate Warne
  8. Dave Toschi
  9. Jay J. Armes
  10. Robert Ressler

1. Sherlock Holmes

Sherlock Holmes is perhaps the most famous fictional detective of all time, but he was inspired by a real-life detective named Dr. Joseph Bell. One of his most famous cases was the Hound of the Baskervilles, in which he solved the mystery of a cursed family and a terrifying spectral hound. Although Holmes had a near-perfect record of solving cases, he was unable to solve the mystery of the Red-Headed League.

2. Allan Pinkerton

Allan Pinkerton, a Scottish-American detective, established the Pinkerton National Detective Agency. His most famous case was the thwarting of a plot to assassinate Abraham Lincoln during his journey to his inauguration. Pinkerton also investigated the infamous Jesse James gang, but was unable to capture the notorious outlaw.

3. William Burns

William Burns was an American detective who founded the William J. Burns Detective Agency. He solved many high-profile cases during his career, including the infamous murder of the oil magnate Charles Urschel. However, Burns was unable to solve the case of the missing aviator Amelia Earhart.

4. Joseph Wambaugh

Joseph Wambaugh is a retired Los Angeles police officer and bestselling author of crime novels. One of his most famous cases was the Hillside Strangler case, in which he helped catch the notorious serial killers Kenneth Bianchi and Angelo Buono.

5. Frank Abagnale

Frank Abagnale is a former con artist and fraudster who became a highly successful security consultant. His most famous case was his own, as he was able to successfully impersonate a pilot, a doctor, and a lawyer before being caught and serving time in prison.

6. Ed Parker

Ed Parker was an American detective who solved many high-profile cases during his career, including the murder of actress Lana Turner's boyfriend, Johnny Stompanato. However, he was unable to solve the infamous Black Dahlia murder case.

7. Kate Warne

Kate Warne was the first female detective in the United States and worked for the Pinkerton National Detective Agency. Her most famous case was the thwarting of a plot to assassinate President-elect Abraham Lincoln during his journey to Washington, D.C.

8. Dave Toschi

Dave Toschi was a San Francisco detective who worked on the infamous Zodiac Killer case. Although he was instrumental in gathering evidence and investigating suspects, the case remains unsolved to this day.

9. Jay J. Armes

Jay J. Armes is a private detective who gained notoriety for his unorthodox methods, including his use of prosthetic limbs and a custom-made helicopter. One of his most famous cases was the solving of the kidnapping of Marlon Brando's son, Christian.

10. Robert Ressler

Robert Ressler was an FBI agent who is credited with coining the term "serial killer" and developing the FBI's behavioral analysis unit. He was involved in many high-profile cases, including the investigation of the Green River Killer and the BTK Killer.

Conclusion:

These 10 real detectives have all left their mark on the world of crime solving, with their famous cases and unsolved mysteries still intriguing people to this day.

While some of their cases may remain unsolved, their dedication and skill in solving crimes have inspired countless others to pursue careers in law enforcement and criminal justice.
